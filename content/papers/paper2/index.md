##### Abstract

This paper replicates and extends Tenreyro and Thwaites (2016, *AEJ: Macroeconomics*) on state-dependent monetary policy transmission, expanding the sample from 1969Q1–2002Q4 to 1969Q1–2019Q4. The 17-year extension covers the 2008Q4–2015Q4 zero lower bound period and the subsequent policy normalization. Methodological contributions include a cumulative local-projection specification following Barnichon and Brownlees (2019) and Montiel Olea and Plagborg-Møller (2021), replacing Tenreyro and Thwaites's stacked horizon-specific approach. The FFR asymmetry rejects symmetric transmission at the 1% level in both the original and extended samples (p = 0.006). Real-side effects on GDP and unemployment do not tighten with the extended data. An Andrews (1993) sup-Wald break test finds no statistically significant structural break at GFC candidate dates.

---

##### Methods

- Smooth-transition state-dependent local projections
- Romer-Romer (2004) narrative monetary shocks with Acosta (2019) extension
- Cumulative-LP specification (Barnichon-Brownlees 2019; Montiel Olea-Plagborg-Møller 2021)
- Newey-West and Driscoll-Kraay HAC inference
- Block bootstrap confidence bands
- Andrews (1993) sup-Wald structural break test
