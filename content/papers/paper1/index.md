##### Abstract

This paper studies equity market volatility responses to 44 U.S.-China trade policy announcements across two Trump administrations (2018–2026). Combining NYSE TAQ Millisecond data for 12 U.S. ETFs with RESSET high-frequency data for 7 Chinese ETFs, I construct realized volatility and cross-market spillover measures exploiting non-overlapping trading hours. U.S. intraday abnormal returns are approximately three times larger in Term 2 (1.15% vs. 0.39% in a two-hour window), while Chinese markets show attenuated reactions. A cross-market difference-in-differences yields a 1.95 percentage point gap at the event-day level. The empirical pattern is interpreted as differential belief revision across markets, drawing on the theoretical frameworks of Pastor and Veronesi (2012) and Kozlowski, Veldkamp, and Venkateswaran (2020).

##### Methods

- High-frequency event study with permutation inference
- Heterogeneous Autoregressive Realized Volatility (HAR-RV) models
- Barndorff-Nielsen and Shephard (2006) jump tests
- Boehmer-Musumeci-Poulsen / Bremer-Zhang event-study test statistics
- Cross-market difference-in-differences
- Sectoral panel analysis with China-exposure metrics
